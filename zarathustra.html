Thus Spoke Zarathustra

This is one of Nietzsche's more famous works

He makes his famous proclomation "God is Dead!" and he also discusses the ubermensch

The "ubermensch" is one of Nietzsche's most prophetic and relevant concepts.

Nietzsche's main character is man name Zarathustra who comes down from a mountain after thinking about God

He comes down to the village and says "God does not exist!"

Although Zarathustra denies the existence of God, he warns the villagers

against the dangers of nihilism and becoming "the last man." The last man is

a man who has no purpose and becomes engulfed in consumption and apathy.


This work is famous for Nietzche's idea of will to power.

This is about man's struggle against his general environment and his desire

to overcome obstacles. His character Zarathustra embraces eternity and the enternal

nature of will to power. There is also the idea of eternal reoccurence.

Man is in a never ending state of desire and unease.
