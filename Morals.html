The Geneology of Morals

In this work, Nietzsche aggressively criticizes Judeo-Christian principles and morality.

It is no secret at this point he does not like Christianity! He feels Christianity enslaves people

by disguising weakness as virtues. He also cites how guilt comes from the idea of enjoying

another one's suffering. Culture and reality are described as human made ideas. From this abstracted sense of reality

and culture come morals that are invented as well. Nietszche argues that most systems of ethics are designed to make the noble

class look good and the lower class look bad. The seperating of the ordinary from the normal is called the "Pathos of Existense"
